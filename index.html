US Market Opportunity

There is already real, growing US demand — with no US store behind it.

Pedestal sells from European and international Shopify storefronts, with no US-localized store and no US-targeted ad spend. Even so, American shoppers keep finding us — and the trend is accelerating. This is a 24-month snapshot of that latent demand: how much, where, and how fast it's growing — with bot & AI-agent traffic filtered out so you're looking at real people.

The opportunity

  • Demand exists before we've even shown up. 158,762 real US visitor sessions in 24 months, entirely organic — no US storefront, no US ad accounts, prices in EUR, shipping from Europe.
  • It's growing fast. Real US sessions more than doubled (+126%), with a clear step-up from October 2025 onward — momentum a US partner is set to convert.
  • It's concentrated. California, New York and Texas alone are ~38% of US demand; the top metros are Los Angeles, New York City, the Bay Area, Chicago, Seattle, Dallas and Atlanta — a focused launch/fulfilment map.
  • The friction is obvious. Today these visitors hit EUR pricing and European shipping. A US store, local fulfilment and targeted marketing remove exactly the barriers this traffic is already pushing through.

How to read this — and the bot/AI caveat

Source: Shopify "Sessions by location" across all 11 Pedestal storefronts, United States only, monthly, Jun 2024–May 2026.

Bots & AI agents flagged out. ~48% of raw "US sessions" came from cloud-datacenter locations — Council Bluffs IA (Google), Ashburn VA (AWS), North Bergen NJ, Forest City NC (Meta), Prineville OR. These are search/AI crawlers, on-demand AI fetchers (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI) and monitoring bots; they geolocate to the server, not a person, so they're excluded from the real-customer view shown here. The full all-traffic figures (state, city) are included for completeness, and the chart above shows the split. Real humans who arrive from an AI answer (a person clicking through) are counted normally, at their real location.
Prepared June 2026 · data through May 2026 · figures are website sessions (visits), not orders.

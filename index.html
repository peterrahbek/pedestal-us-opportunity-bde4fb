The State of US Demand · 2026

The state of US demand is strong — and we haven't even arrived.

Pedestal has never run a US storefront, localized a price, shipped to the US, or bought a paid US ad. Yet over the past 24 months the United States has become one of our largest sources of demand — almost entirely organic — and it is accelerating. This is the state of that demand: how large, how concentrated, and how fast it is compounding — measured across all 11 storefronts, with bot and AI-agent traffic stripped out so every number reflects a real person.

Where we stand

  • The US is already a major market. 158,762 real US visitor sessions in 24 months — and Pedestal doesn't even ship there. No US storefront, no US ad accounts, prices in EUR, no US delivery. They can't buy yet, and they keep coming.
  • Demand is compounding, not drifting. Real US sessions grew +73% year over year — about 100,000 in the last 12 months, up from ~58,000 in the prior 12 — with a decisive step-up from October 2025. A log-linear trend across all 24 months implies the same ≈70%/year.
  • It is geographically concentrated. California, New York and Texas are ~38% of the total; the metro map centers on Los Angeles, New York, the Bay Area, Chicago, Seattle, Dallas and Atlanta — a tight, addressable footprint.
  • Every barrier is still in place — and it climbs anyway. Today a US visitor lands on EUR prices with no way to get the product delivered. A US store, local fulfilment and targeted marketing turn traffic that currently can't convert into revenue; the ceiling sits well above the chart.
  • And we already have a lever that works. A light-touch program of just five gifted US creators (from October 2025) coincided with a marked, durable step-up in US sessions — an early signal that a small, deliberate push moves this market fast.

Methodology & data integrity

Source: Shopify "Sessions by location" across all 11 Pedestal storefronts, United States only, monthly, Jun 2024–May 2026.

Bots & AI agents flagged out. ~48% of raw "US sessions" came from cloud-datacenter locations — Council Bluffs IA (Google), Ashburn VA (AWS), North Bergen NJ, Forest City NC (Meta), Prineville OR. These are search/AI crawlers, on-demand AI fetchers (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI) and monitoring bots; they geolocate to the server, not a person, so they're excluded from the real-customer view shown here. The full all-traffic figures (state, city) are included for completeness, and the chart above shows the split. Real humans who arrive from an AI answer (a person clicking through) are counted normally, at their real location.

Growth is measured year-over-year — the most recent 12 months against the prior 12 — to neutralize seasonality; a log-linear trend across all 24 months implies the same ≈70%/year.
